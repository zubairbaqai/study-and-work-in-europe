## The universities at a glance

Italy has a mix of large public universities, a handful of small elite schools, and one famous private university. Here are the ones most international students end up looking at.

| University | Known for |
|---|---|
| **Politecnico di Milano** | Italy's top technical university: engineering, architecture, and design. Consistently the highest-ranked Italian university for those fields. |
| **Bocconi University (Milan)** | Elite private university for economics, finance, and management. Heavily international, strong recruiting pipeline into consulting and banking. |
| **University of Bologna** | The oldest university in continuous operation in the world (founded 1088). Broad excellence across law, humanities, economics, and sciences, with a large English-taught catalogue. |
| **Sapienza University of Rome** | One of Europe's largest universities. Strong in classics and archaeology, physics, engineering, and medicine. |
| **University of Padua** | A historic research university (Galileo taught here). Strong in the sciences, medicine, and psychology. |
| **Politecnico di Torino** | Turin's technical university: engineering and architecture, with close ties to the automotive and aerospace industry in the region. |
| **University of Milan (Statale)** | Large public university, strong in medicine, law, the sciences, and the humanities. |
| **Scuola Normale Superiore di Pisa** | Tiny, highly selective elite school for sciences and humanities, paired with the broader **University of Pisa** (strong in physics, maths, computer science, and engineering). |
| **University of Trento** | Smaller, research-intensive university that punches above its weight in computer science, physics, and economics. |
| **University of Florence** | Broad public university with notable architecture, art history, and engineering programs. |
| **University of Naples Federico II** | The largest university in southern Italy and one of the oldest public universities, with strong engineering and medicine. |
| **Ca' Foscari University of Venice** | Known for languages and Asian/Middle Eastern studies, plus economics and management. |
| **University of Trieste** | Strong in physics and the sciences, sitting next to a cluster of international research institutes. |

If you are choosing between cities as much as universities, that is normal in Italy. Milan and Turin lean technical and connected to industry, Bologna and Padua are classic student cities, Rome and Naples are big and historic, and Pisa, Trento, and Trieste are smaller research hubs.

## How to apply

The process for non-EU students applying from abroad runs through two channels at the same time: the university's own admission system, and the government pre-enrollment system.

1. **Choose your program** and check that university's admission page for its specific requirements and deadlines. Each university sets its own rules on top of the national procedure.
2. **Apply for admission** directly to the university (most run an online portal). For competitive programs you may need to pass an entrance test first (see below).
3. **Pre-enroll on Universitaly.** Non-EU students applying from abroad complete a pre-enrollment application on the national portal at [universitaly.it](https://www.universitaly.it). This is the step that links your university place to your visa application.
4. **Go through the Italian embassy or consulate** in your country. The consulate validates your pre-enrollment and is where you later apply for the student visa. Pre-enrollment and the visa are handled together through this office, so the consulate that covers your region is your main point of contact.

For the official, country-by-country version of this procedure, start at [studyinitaly.esteri.it](https://studyinitaly.esteri.it), which is run by the Italian Ministry of Foreign Affairs and is the authoritative source for international applicants.

## Tests you might need

Italy uses a few standard entrance tests. Which one applies depends entirely on the program.

- **IMAT** — the International Medical Admissions Test, used for English-taught Medicine and Surgery degrees. It is the gateway exam for those programs; check each medical school's page for the year's IMAT registration window.
- **TOLC** — a family of online admission tests run by **CISIA** ([cisia.it](https://www.cisia.it)). Many bachelor's programs in engineering, economics, sciences, and other fields require a TOLC (for example TOLC-I for engineering, TOLC-E for economics). You usually take it before or early in the application process.
- **Program-specific exams** — some programs, especially competitive master's at Bocconi or Politecnico di Milano, run their own admission assessments instead of or in addition to the above. Always read the program page.

## Language requirements

- **Italian-taught programs** generally expect around **B2-level Italian**, often proven with a recognised certificate (CILS, CELI, PLIDA, or an equivalent). The exact level and accepted certificates vary by university.
- **English-taught programs** generally expect proof of English, commonly **IELTS or TOEFL** at a level the program sets. Some programs accept other certificates or waive the requirement for native speakers or prior English-medium study.

Check the specific score and certificate each program lists, because there is no single national cutoff.

## Getting your foreign qualification recognised

A university outside Italy issued your high school diploma or degree, so Italy needs a way to compare it to its own qualifications. Two main routes exist:

- **Dichiarazione di Valore (Declaration of Value)** — a document issued by the Italian embassy or consulate that describes your foreign qualification and its status in your home country.
- **CIMEA Statements** — CIMEA (the Italian ENIC-NARIC centre) issues a **Statement of Comparability** and related verification statements that many universities now accept in place of, or alongside, the Dichiarazione di Valore. See [cimea.it](https://www.cimea.it).

Which one your university wants varies, so confirm with the admissions office before you pay for either. Many universities have moved toward accepting CIMEA statements because they are faster to obtain.

## Famous English-taught programs

Italy has expanded its English-taught offering a lot. This is a sample to show the range, not a complete list. Always confirm the current catalogue on each university's own site.

| University | Examples of English-taught programs |
|---|---|
| **Politecnico di Milano** | Many master's in engineering (mechanical, computer science, management) and in architecture and design taught fully in English. |
| **Bocconi (Milan)** | Master's in finance, management, economics, and data science taught in English, plus an English-taught BSc track. |
| **University of Bologna** | One of the largest English-taught catalogues in Italy across economics, engineering, the sciences, and humanities. |
| **University of Padua** | A growing set of English-taught master's in the sciences, engineering, and economics. |
| **University of Trento** | English-taught master's in computer science, data science, and economics. |
| **Various medical schools** | English-taught Medicine and Surgery degrees admitted via the IMAT exam. |
