# Scholarships and Financial Aid

The main ways international students fund study in Italy, from the large income-based regional grants to government and merit scholarships. The single most important thing to understand here is the ISEE income assessment, because it drives both your tuition and your eligibility for the biggest scholarships.

## ISEE and ISEE Parificato: the income assessment

**ISEE** (Indicatore della Situazione Economica Equivalente) is Italy's standard measure of household economic situation. It combines income and assets into a single figure, and that figure decides two big things for a student: how much tuition you pay at a public university, and whether you qualify for a right-to-study scholarship.

For families whose income and assets are outside Italy, there is the **ISEE Parificato** (also called ISEE for non-resident families). It is the equivalent calculation based on documents from your home country, usually arranged through a CAF (a tax-assistance centre) in Italy. Most non-EU students who want income-based tuition or a DSU scholarship will need an ISEE Parificato.

Sort this out early. Both your tuition rate and your scholarship application depend on it, and it takes time to gather and certify the foreign documents.

## Regional "right to study" (DSU) scholarships

The largest scholarships in Italy are the **Diritto allo Studio Universitario (DSU)** grants. These are not run by the universities or the national government but by **regional right-to-study bodies**, and they are income-based via your ISEE or ISEE Parificato, with a merit/credit requirement to keep them in later years.

Each region has its own body, for example:

| Region | Body |
|---|---|
| Lazio (Rome) | **DiSCo Lazio** |
| Lombardy (Milan) | regional right-to-study services (e.g. through the universities / **ERGO**-type bodies) |
| Piedmont (Turin) | **EDISU Piemonte** |
| Emilia-Romagna (Bologna) | **ER.GO** |
| Tuscany (Florence, Pisa, Siena) | **DSU Toscana** |

A DSU scholarship is usually a package rather than just cash. It commonly includes a **tuition fee waiver**, a **cash grant**, and **access to subsidised university canteens and student housing**. That combination is what makes it the most valuable aid most students can get, and for low-income students it can bring the real cost of study close to zero.

Because the rules, income thresholds, and amounts are set per region and change each year, apply through the right regional body for your university's city and read that year's call ("bando"). Search for the body by name (DiSCo Lazio, EDISU Piemonte, ER.GO, DSU Toscana) to reach the official site.

## Italian Government Scholarships (MAECI)

The Italian Ministry of Foreign Affairs (**MAECI**) offers scholarships for international students and Italians abroad. These typically cover a monthly allowance and health cover for a set period, and they run on an annual call with a fixed application window. The amounts and eligibility are set each year, so check the current call rather than assuming last year's terms. The authoritative page is [studyinitaly.esteri.it](https://studyinitaly.esteri.it).

Italian government and "right to study" applications almost always ask for a CV in **Europass** format. If you don't have one, the [free Europass converter](https://www.prezumi.com/tools/europass-converter) turns a resume into the EU standard layout, and [student CV examples](https://www.prezumi.com/blog/student-cv-examples) show what an education-led CV looks like — the [job-hunt section](../job-hunt/) has the full rundown.

## Invest Your Talent in Italy

**Invest Your Talent in Italy** is a government-backed program that offers scholarships to international students from selected countries for master's programs in fields like engineering, economics, management, and design, usually with an internship component at an Italian company. Eligible countries and participating universities are set each year. Check the official program details through [studyinitaly.esteri.it](https://studyinitaly.esteri.it).

## University merit scholarships

Some universities run their own merit-based aid:

- **Bocconi** offers merit awards and need-based fee reductions for international students, including partial and full tuition waivers for strong applicants.
- **Politecnico di Milano** offers merit-based scholarships for international students on its English-taught master's programs.

These are decided in the admission process, so read each university's scholarship page when you apply and note its separate deadline.

## Erasmus+

**Erasmus+** is the EU mobility program. It mainly funds study or traineeship periods abroad once you are already enrolled at a participating university, with a grant toward mobility costs. If you are enrolled in an Italian university, your international office handles Erasmus+ placements. Some Erasmus Mundus joint master's also fund full degrees, with their own admissions.

## A note on amounts

Scholarship amounts, ISEE thresholds, and the SSN/health terms attached to government grants change every year and differ by region and program. As of 2024/2025 these schemes were all active, but do not quote a figure from memory in an application. Always read the current year's official call and confirm numbers on the source below before you rely on them.
