# Studying Data Science, Machine Learning, and AI in Germany and Italy

Data science and AI are fast-growing fields with a lot of new English-taught Master's programs, and a few European universities have become genuine research hubs for machine learning. This file covers where to study, what to look for in a program, and the job outlook.

## What "data science and AI" covers

These programs sit between computer science, statistics, and a domain. Names vary — Data Science, Machine Learning, Artificial Intelligence, Data Engineering, Computational Statistics — and the content ranges from heavily mathematical and research-oriented to applied and industry-facing. Read each program's module list rather than trusting the title, because two programs with the same name can be very different.

## Germany

Germany has a strong AI/ML research base, and Tübingen in particular has become a recognised European hub.

### Top schools

| University | Known for |
| --- | --- |
| TU Munich (TUM) | Top CS with deep AI/ML research and an industry ecosystem |
| LMU Munich | Strong statistics and machine learning; Munich's AI scene |
| University of Tübingen | A major AI/ML cluster (the "Cyber Valley" area) and an [ELLIS](https://ellis.eu) unit; strong ML research |
| Saarland University | Excellent CS/AI with surrounding research institutes (Max Planck, DFKI) |
| TU Darmstadt | Strong AI and machine learning research |

Tübingen is the name to know for machine-learning research: it anchors a regional AI cluster and is part of the **ELLIS** network of European ML labs. Check the network at [ellis.eu](https://ellis.eu) for which groups and programs are affiliated.

### How to apply

Apply through [uni-assist](https://www.uni-assist.de) or directly, depending on the program. Data science and AI Master's are often competitive and frequently demand a quantitative background — substantial maths, statistics, and programming in your prior degree. Expect to show a recognised relevant Bachelor's, transcripts, English proof, and a statement of purpose, sometimes with specific prerequisite modules. Confirm requirements and deadlines per program via the university, [DAAD](https://www.daad.de), and the [Germany admissions guide](../germany/admissions.md).

## Italy

Italy has expanded its data science and AI offering quickly, with strong programs at the polytechnics and research universities, including elite research institutions.

### Top schools

| University | Known for |
| --- | --- |
| Politecnico di Milano | Strong data science, ML, and engineering-oriented AI programs |
| University of Bologna | Large CS and AI faculty; English-taught data programs |
| Sapienza University of Rome | Big faculty with data science and AI offerings |
| University of Pisa / Scuola Normale Superiore | CS tradition plus, at Scuola Normale, an elite, research-intensive environment |
| University of Trento | Research-focused, international, with data and AI programs |

Pisa is a long-standing Italian CS centre, and the **Scuola Normale Superiore** in Pisa is a small, highly selective research institution rather than a typical university; programs there are research-intensive and competitive.

### How to apply

For English-taught Master's, apply on the university's portal; non-EU students from abroad also pre-enroll on [Universitaly](https://www.universitaly.it) via the consulate. You'll generally need a recognised quantitative Bachelor's, transcripts, and English proof, with some programs assessing background or running rounds with their own deadlines. The full procedure is in the [Italy admissions guide](../italy/admissions.md), and the national overview is [studyinitaly.esteri.it](https://studyinitaly.esteri.it). Verify deadlines on the program page.

## What to look for in a program

- **Mathematical depth.** Check how much linear algebra, probability, statistics, and optimisation the program assumes and teaches. Research-track ML demands more than applied data science.
- **Prerequisites.** Many programs require a quantitative Bachelor's and specific prior modules. Confirm you qualify before applying.
- **Research vs. applied.** Decide whether you want a research path (toward a PhD or research role) or an applied, industry-facing one, and pick programs accordingly.
- **Affiliations.** Networks like [ELLIS](https://ellis.eu) and links to research institutes signal a strong ML environment.

## How to choose

For research-focused machine learning, Tübingen, Saarland, TUM, and TU Darmstadt in Germany, and Scuola Normale/Pisa and Trento in Italy, are the natural shortlist. For applied, industry-facing data science, TUM and LMU in Germany and Politecnico di Milano and Bologna in Italy fit well. Then filter by the maths level, the prerequisites you meet, and the teaching language. The wider context on each university is in [Germany universities](../germany/universities.md) and [Italy universities](../italy/universities.md).

## Job outlook

Demand for data and AI skills is high in both countries. Germany hires across IT and data roles and offers a structured post-study route — an 18-month residence permit and the EU Blue Card ([working in Germany](../germany/working-and-jobs.md)). Italy's data and tech roles cluster around Milan and a few hubs, with a study-to-work conversion route ([working in Italy](../italy/working-and-jobs.md)). International firms often hire in English, while the local language opens up the rest of the market.
