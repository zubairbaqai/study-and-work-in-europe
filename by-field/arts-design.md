# Studying Arts and Design in Germany and Italy

Art, design, and architecture work differently from most other fields: admission usually turns on a portfolio rather than only on grades or a test, and the famous schools are art academies and design institutes as much as universities. This file covers where to study, what's expected, and how to apply.

## How arts and design admissions work

For fine art, design, and architecture, the **portfolio** is usually the centre of the application, sometimes alongside an entrance exam, an interview, or a practical test. Public art academies often have very few places and select heavily on the portfolio. Some private design schools are easier to enter but charge significant fees. Programs run in German, Italian, or English depending on the school, with more English at Master's level and at the international private schools.

## Germany

Germany has prestigious public art and design schools, plus architecture inside its technical universities.

### Top schools

| Institution | Known for |
| --- | --- |
| Universität der Künste Berlin (UdK) | One of the largest arts universities in Europe; fine art, design, music |
| Bauhaus-Universität Weimar | The Bauhaus heritage; design, architecture, media, art |
| Hochschule für Gestaltung (HfG) Offenbach / Karlsruhe | Respected design schools (HfG) |
| TU Munich (TUM) | Architecture within a top technical university |
| RWTH Aachen | Architecture alongside its engineering strength |

UdK Berlin and Bauhaus Weimar are the marquee names for art and design. For architecture specifically, the technical universities (TUM, RWTH, and others) house strong programs.

### How to apply

Art and design schools run their own admissions and almost always require a portfolio, often followed by an entrance exam or interview; deadlines for portfolio submission can be early and differ from standard university deadlines. Architecture at a technical university may go through the usual route ([uni-assist](https://www.uni-assist.de) or direct) but can still ask for a portfolio. Language requirements vary — German for many programs, English for some. Confirm the portfolio brief, the entrance procedure, and deadlines on the school's own page, and see [DAAD](https://www.daad.de) and the [Germany admissions guide](../germany/admissions.md) for the general process.

## Italy

Italy is a world centre for design and architecture, led by Politecnico di Milano, and has historic fine-art academies.

### Top schools

| Institution | Type | Known for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Politecnico di Milano | Public | World-leading design and architecture, broad and internationally ranked |
| NABA (Nuova Accademia di Belle Arti) | Private | International art and design academy in Milan |
| Domus Academy | Private | Postgraduate design school in Milan |
| Accademia di Belle Arti (Florence, Venice, Rome, etc.) | Public academies | Historic fine-art academies in Italy's art cities |
| IUAV (Venice) | Public | Architecture and design |

Politecnico di Milano's design and architecture schools are among the best in the world. For fine art, Italy's **Accademia di Belle Arti** in cities like Florence and Venice carry centuries of tradition. NABA and Domus Academy are well-known private, international options, with fees to match.

### How to apply

Universities and public academies follow the Italian admissions route, and non-EU students from abroad also pre-enroll on [Universitaly](https://www.universitaly.it) via the consulate; private schools (NABA, Domus) run their own admissions and fees. Most programs require a portfolio, and some require an entrance test or interview. The general procedure, including the consulate and recognition steps, is in the [Italy admissions guide](../italy/admissions.md), and the national overview is [studyinitaly.esteri.it](https://studyinitaly.esteri.it). Confirm the portfolio brief and deadlines on the school's page.

| | Germany | Italy |

| Fine art | UdK Berlin, Bauhaus Weimar | Accademia di Belle Arti (Florence, Venice, Rome) |

## Portfolio and entrance requirements

The portfolio is usually decisive, so treat it as the main project of your application. A few general points, with the caveat that each school sets its own brief:

- **Read the specific brief.** Schools specify how many works, in what format, and sometimes around a theme. Follow it exactly.
- **Show range and a point of view.** Selection panels look for both technical skill and a coherent personal direction.
- **Watch the portfolio deadline.** It is often earlier than, and separate from, the general application deadline.
- **Some programs add a test or interview.** Architecture programs at technical universities may also have an entrance test; confirm what applies.

Do not rely on a general description for your school's exact requirements — confirm them on the official program page before you build the portfolio.

## How to choose

For design and architecture, Politecnico di Milano in Italy is a global first choice, with UdK Berlin, Bauhaus Weimar, and architecture at TUM or RWTH on the German side. For fine art, Italy's historic academies and Germany's UdK and Bauhaus are the names to weigh. Then decide between public schools (low fees, very competitive portfolios, often local-language) and private international schools like NABA and Domus (higher fees, more English, broader entry). The wider context on each institution is in [Germany universities](../germany/universities.md) and [Italy universities](../italy/universities.md).
