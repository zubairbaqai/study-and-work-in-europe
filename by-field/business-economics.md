# Studying Business, Economics, and Finance in Germany and Italy

Both countries have business schools and economics faculties with international reputations, a strong supply of English-taught Master's, and clear routes into the financial and corporate job market. This file covers where to study and how to apply.

## The shape of business education here

Business and economics come in several forms: a Bachelor's, a specialised Master's (in finance, management, economics, marketing, and so on), and the MBA, which is usually a postgraduate program aimed at people with work experience. Germany has both strong public university faculties and a set of well-known private business schools. Italy's standout is a private university, Bocconi, alongside strong public options. English-taught programs are common at Master's and MBA level.

## Germany

Germany mixes excellent public faculties with respected private business schools.

### Top schools

| Institution | Type | Known for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| University of Mannheim | Public | Germany's leading business and economics faculty, US-style structure |
| LMU Munich | Public | Strong economics and management in a major city |
| Goethe University Frankfurt | Public | Economics and finance in Germany's financial capital |
| WHU – Otto Beisheim School of Management | Private | Well-known private business school, strong management/finance |
| ESMT Berlin | Private | International management school with a strong MBA |
| University of Cologne | Public | Large, respected economics and business faculty |

Mannheim is the first name for business and economics at a German public university. Frankfurt's advantage is location in the banking sector. WHU and ESMT are the private schools most often mentioned for management and MBA-style programs.

### How to apply

Public universities take applications through [uni-assist](https://www.uni-assist.de) or directly; private schools (WHU, ESMT) run their own admissions and have their own fees, which are substantial compared with public tuition. Expect to submit a recognised Bachelor's, transcripts, English proof, often a CV and motivation letter, and for some Master's and MBAs a GMAT or GRE score. Requirements, fees, and deadlines vary widely by program and change — confirm on the institution's site, [DAAD](https://www.daad.de), and the [Germany admissions guide](../germany/admissions.md).

## Italy

Italy's business education is led by Bocconi, with several other strong options.

### Top schools

| Institution | Type | Known for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Bocconi University (Milan) | Private | Italy's elite business and economics school, internationally ranked |
| LUISS (Rome) | Private | Well-regarded business, economics, and political science |
| Politecnico di Milano / MIP (POLIMI Graduate School of Management) | Public/business school | Management engineering and MBA programs |
| University of Bologna | Public | Large, respected economics and management faculty |

Bocconi is the clear flagship, with a strong international reputation in business, economics, and finance, and a large set of English-taught programs. As a private university its fees are higher than Italian public universities, and they're often income-linked — confirm the current figures on Bocconi's own site.

### How to apply

Private schools like Bocconi and LUISS run their own admissions, often with rounds, their own tests or assessments, and their own deadlines. Public options (Bologna, Politecnico/MIP) follow the university route; non-EU students from abroad also pre-enroll on [Universitaly](https://www.universitaly.it) via the consulate. Expect a recognised Bachelor's, transcripts, English proof, a CV and motivation letter, and possibly GMAT/GRE. The general procedure is in the [Italy admissions guide](../italy/admissions.md), and the national overview is [studyinitaly.esteri.it](https://studyinitaly.esteri.it). Verify each program's deadlines and fees on its own page.

## GMAT, GRE, and admissions tests

Many competitive Master's in management and finance, and most MBAs, ask for a **GMAT** or **GRE** score, and some private schools run their own admissions test or assessment instead. Whether a test is required, and whether the school accepts the GMAT, the GRE, or its own test, is set per program and changes. Do not assume — check the specific program's admissions page. The same applies to whether a minimum score or band is expected.

## How to choose

For a respected, low-fee public degree in business or economics, Mannheim and Frankfurt in Germany and Bologna in Italy are the starting points. For an internationally ranked brand and a strong recruiting pipeline, Bocconi in Italy and WHU or ESMT in Germany are the names to weigh, with higher fees in return. For an MBA, compare ESMT, WHU, Bocconi, and Politecnico/MIP on cost, format, and where their graduates get hired. The wider strengths of each university are in [Germany universities](../germany/universities.md) and [Italy universities](../italy/universities.md).

## Job outlook

Business and economics graduates target consulting, finance, corporate roles, and industry. Frankfurt is the obvious base for finance in Germany, and Milan for finance and corporate roles in Italy. Germany offers an 18-month post-study residence permit and the EU Blue Card for qualified roles ([working in Germany](../germany/working-and-jobs.md)); Italy has a study-to-work conversion route ([working in Italy](../italy/working-and-jobs.md)). For client-facing and many corporate roles, the local language matters a great deal, so factor German or Italian into your plan early.
