# Studying Computer Science in Germany and Italy

Computer science and informatics are among the strongest reasons to study in Germany or Italy: solid technical universities, a large number of English-taught Master's, and a job market that hires graduates quickly. This file covers where to study and how to apply.

## The shape of CS in Europe

Most top CS programs at Master's level are taught in English, so you can study without prior German or Italian. Bachelor's programs are more often in the local language. Many German and Italian universities also run a strong research culture in CS, which matters if you're aiming at AI, security, systems, or a doctorate later.

## Germany

Germany has several genuinely strong CS faculties, and one of them — Saarland — has a research reputation out of proportion to its size.

### Top CS schools

| University | Known for |
| --- | --- |
| TU Munich (TUM) | Top-rated CS, strong across systems, algorithms, and AI; big industry ecosystem |
| Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT) | Excellent CS and informatics, plus a national research centre |
| Saarland University | Outstanding CS and AI reputation; surrounded by major research institutes (Max Planck, CISPA, DFKI) |
| RWTH Aachen | Strong CS alongside its famous engineering |
| TU Berlin | Solid CS in a big-city tech and startup scene |
| TU Darmstadt | Well known for CS and especially IT security |

Saarland is worth singling out: despite being a smaller university, its computer science cluster — with Max Planck institutes, the CISPA security centre, and DFKI nearby — gives it a research weight that punches far above the university's overall size.

### How to apply

Apply either through [uni-assist](https://www.uni-assist.de) or directly to the university, depending on the program. For a Master's you'll generally need a recognised CS or related Bachelor's, transcripts, English proof for English-taught programs, and often a statement of purpose; some programs check for specific prior coursework (maths, theory, programming). Deadlines and exact requirements are per program and change yearly — confirm on the university's page, [DAAD](https://www.daad.de), and the [Germany admissions guide](../germany/admissions.md).

## Italy

Italy's CS strength sits at the polytechnics and several large research universities, with a good supply of English-taught Master's.

### Top CS schools

| University | Known for |
| --- | --- |
| Politecnico di Milano | Italy's top technical school; strong CS and computer engineering, English Master's |
| University of Bologna | Large, well-regarded CS, historic university |
| Sapienza University of Rome | Big CS faculty, strong in several areas including AI |
| University of Pisa | A traditional CS heartland in Italy with strong research |
| University of Trento | Modern, research-focused CS with English programs and international cohorts |

Pisa deserves a mention for its long history in Italian computer science, and Trento is popular with international students for its English-taught, research-oriented programs.

### How to apply

For English-taught Master's you apply on the university's admissions portal; non-EU applicants from abroad also complete pre-enrollment on [Universitaly](https://www.universitaly.it) via the Italian consulate. Expect to submit a recognised Bachelor's, transcripts, and English proof, with some programs evaluating your academic background or assessing applicants in rounds. The full procedure is in the [Italy admissions guide](../italy/admissions.md), and the national overview for international students is [studyinitaly.esteri.it](https://studyinitaly.esteri.it). Verify deadlines on the program page.

## How to choose

If research strength and a future doctorate matter most, Saarland, KIT, and TUM in Germany, and Pisa and Trento in Italy, are natural starting points. If you want a degree plus a big tech and startup ecosystem around you, TU Berlin and Munich in Germany and Milano in Italy fit. After that, filter by which programs run in English (most Master's do, fewer Bachelor's) and which match your prior coursework. The wider strengths of each university are in [Germany universities](../germany/universities.md) and [Italy universities](../italy/universities.md).

## Job market

CS graduates are in strong demand in both countries. Germany has explicit shortages in IT and a clear post-study route — an 18-month residence permit to find a job and the EU Blue Card for qualified roles ([working in Germany](../germany/working-and-jobs.md)). Italy's tech sector, concentrated around Milan and a few other hubs, hires graduates too, with a study-to-work conversion route ([working in Italy](../italy/working-and-jobs.md)). English is often enough to get a first software job at international firms, but the local language broadens your options and is usually needed outside large multinationals.

## Preparing your application

A clean, software-readable CV matters more in tech than almost anywhere, because large employers screen with applicant tracking systems. You can [test what a parser actually reads from your PDF](https://www.prezumi.com/tools/ats-resume-checker) for free before you apply.

- [ATS-friendly resumes](../job-hunt/ats-friendly-resume.md) — keep your resume readable by the software that screens it first.
- [Europass CV](../job-hunt/europass-cv.md) — the EU standard, often expected for scholarships and Italian applications.
- [German & Italian CV norms](../job-hunt/application-cv-by-country.md) — what differs between a German and an Italian CV.
