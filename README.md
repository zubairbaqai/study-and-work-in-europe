# Study & Work in Germany and Italy

**An open, practical handbook for international students: choosing a university, getting admitted, funding it, getting your visa, living there, and landing a job — for two of Europe's best destinations to study for free or near-free.**

Germany and Italy are two of the few places where a strong degree can cost almost nothing in tuition, and where an international student has a real path to staying and working afterward. The information to make that happen is real, but it's scattered across government portals, university sites, expat forums, and a lot of outdated blog posts.

This handbook pulls the practical parts into one place: which universities to consider and how to apply, where the scholarships are, what living actually costs, how the student visa and post-study work routes function, and how to prepare the application documents that admissions committees and employers expect. Every figure links to an official source, because the numbers change and you should always confirm the current one.

It's open. If something here is wrong or out of date, [open an issue or a pull request](CONTRIBUTING.md).

## Germany vs. Italy at a glance

A starting orientation. The detail (and the sources) live in the linked files.

| | 🇩🇪 Germany | 🇮🇹 Italy |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Public tuition | Free or near-free in most states (semester fee only); some non-EU fees in Baden-Württemberg | Income-based via ISEE; low, and free/near-free for low-income students |
| Main application route | [uni-assist](germany/admissions.md) or direct to the university | [Universitaly](italy/admissions.md) pre-enrollment + consulate |
| Language for study | German programs (TestDaF/DSH) or English Master's | Italian programs (B2) or English programs |
| Biggest scholarships | [DAAD](germany/scholarships.md), Deutschlandstipendium, foundations | [Regional DSU](italy/scholarships.md), MAECI, Invest Your Talent |
| Proof of funds for visa | [Blocked account (Sperrkonto)](germany/visa-and-blocked-account.md) | [Proof of funds + accommodation](italy/visa.md) |
| Work while studying | Limited days/year ([details](germany/working-and-jobs.md)) | Part-time hours cap ([details](italy/working-and-jobs.md)) |
| After graduation | 18-month residence permit to find a job; EU Blue Card | Study-to-work conversion; post-study permit |
| Expected CV | German Lebenslauf + cover letter | Europass CV |

> These are orientation points, not legal advice. Immigration rules and figures change — always confirm the current detail on the official source linked in each file.

## Why Germany and Italy specifically

- **Cost.** Public universities in both countries are free or income-based, not the five- and six-figure tuition of comparable degrees elsewhere. Your main cost is living, not studying.
- **English-taught options.** Both now run a large and growing number of English-taught Master's programs, so you don't have to be fluent before you arrive — though learning the language transforms your job prospects.
- **A real path to staying.** Germany offers an 18-month post-study residence permit to find work and the EU Blue Card for qualified roles. Italy lets you convert a study permit toward work. Neither treats graduation as the end of your stay.
- **Strong job markets in specific fields.** Engineering, IT, and healthcare in Germany; engineering, design, and manufacturing in northern Italy.

## Build your application documents

Admissions committees, scholarship panels, and employers all read documents — and the format expected in Europe is not the one most international students arrive with. The [job-hunt section](job-hunt/) covers it in depth:

- **A resume that software can actually read.** Large employers screen with applicant tracking systems. [Build a free, ATS-checked resume with Prezumi](https://www.prezumi.com/templates/resumes), or test one you already have with the [free ATS checker](https://www.prezumi.com/tools/ats-resume-checker).
- **A Europass CV when Europe expects it.** Italy and most scholarship applications want the EU standard format. The [free Europass converter](https://www.prezumi.com/tools/europass-converter) turns a resume you have into a Europass-styled CV.
- **Worked examples, if you're starting cold.** Most students arriving in Europe have never written an education-led CV. See [student CV examples](https://www.prezumi.com/blog/student-cv-examples) and, if you have little or no work history yet, [how to write a first CV with no experience](https://www.prezumi.com/blog/first-job-cv-no-experience).
