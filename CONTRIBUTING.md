# Contributing

Thanks for helping keep this handbook accurate. It's meant to be a reference international students can trust, so the bar is "checkable and current," not "more is better."

## What's welcome

- **Corrections and updates.** Fees, deadlines, blocked-account amounts, work-hour limits, and scholarship terms change every year. If a figure is out of date, fix it and link the source you used.
- **New universities, courses, or scholarships.** Add them to the relevant country file with a one-line description and an official link.
- **First-hand process notes.** If you've been through uni-assist, Universitaly, a Sperrkonto, or a permesso di soggiorno, your practical details help.
- **Honest resources and tools.** Genuinely useful, ideally free. One sentence on what it does.
- **Clarity edits.** Shorter, plainer sentences are usually better.

## What gets removed

- Made-up or unsourced figures. Every euro amount, deadline, or limit should link an official source (government, DAAD, uni-assist, Universitaly, MAECI, a university's own page).
- Affiliate or referral spam, and "tools" that are thin lead-gen with no real function.
- Immigration claims stated as certainty. Rules vary by nationality and change often; phrase them as "as of [year], typically… — verify at [source]."
- Keyword-stuffed or AI-generated filler. Write like a person explaining something they actually did.

## How to submit

1. Fork the repo and create a branch.
2. Make your change. Keep the existing tone — plain, direct sentences; no hype.
3. For any figure, hedge it with a year and link the official source.
4. Open a pull request describing what you changed and how you verified it.

Small, specific PRs get merged fastest.
